Weekly % Recurring — by Client Session Number

12-week trend by session cohort: 1st session, 2nd session, 3rd session, and 4th+ sessions (therapy only, excl. psych)

Key insight: The rollout (Apr 9/14) most visibly lifts recurring rates at the 1st session — historically near 0%, now reaching ~3–4%. Even small gains here are meaningful: a provider booking recurring at a client's very first session dramatically reduces early dropout. The 2nd, 3rd, and 4th+ cohorts all show a step-up post-rollout, with 4th+ sessions (established clients) consistently the highest recurring at ~26–29%.

Who's Leaving Without a Next Session? — April 2026 🎯 Key Opportunity

Composition of ~68,000 clients NOT rebooked (no 2nd session within 14 days) in April, by tenure · Source: Looker

This shows who makes up the un-rebooked pool — not the rate at which each group fails to rebook. The 86.5% established clients dominate simply because there are far more of them, and they're likely to return. The red flag is the 11.2% who are brand-new: one session, nothing on the calendar, highest dropout risk. The in-session video prompt A/B test is directly targeting this group.

What Each Slice Means

New clients — 11.2% (~7,600 clients)
Had exactly 1 session. No prior engagement to fall back on. Most likely to fall out of care entirely if not rebooked.
2nd session only — 2.3% (~1,550 clients)
Had exactly 2 sessions. Early in care but have returned once — still at risk without a next booking.
Established (3+ sessions) — 86.5% (~58,800 clients)
Had 3+ sessions. Largest slice because there are far more of them. Strong prior engagement — most likely to return on their own.
